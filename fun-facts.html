Fun Facts

Discover interesting facts about world flags!

The Only Country with a Square Flag

Switzerland and the Vatican City are the only two countries in the world with square flags. Most national flags have a rectangular shape with specific proportions.

The Flag with the Most Colors

Belize's flag contains the most colors of any national flag, with 12 different colors including a complex coat of arms in the center.

Flags That Are Perfectly Symmetrical

Some flags like those of Germany, Belgium, and Chad look the same when flipped horizontally, while others like the flag of Saudi Arabia are designed to be viewed from a specific orientation.

The Color Blue Dominates

Blue is the most commonly used color in national flags, appearing in over 75% of all world flags. Red and white are also very common colors.

Flags with Only Two Colors

Libya's flag from 1977-2011 was a simple rectangle of green color, making it the only national flag with just one color and no design elements.

Evolution of the American Flag

The American flag has had 27 different versions throughout history, changing each time a new state was added. The current version has been in use since 1960.

The Nordic Cross

Five countries (Denmark, Sweden, Norway, Finland, and Iceland) have flags featuring the Nordic Cross, symbolizing their shared cultural heritage.

Flags That Changed Due to Political Events

Many flags have changed due to political events. For example, South Sudan's flag was adopted in 2011 after it became the world's newest country.
